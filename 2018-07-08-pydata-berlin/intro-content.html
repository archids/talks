Python versus R. R versus Python. Why are these two technologies, each an excellent tool in its own right, often viewed as rivals?

Data Science is a field divided. There is a Python camp and a R camp. Each believes that their tool is superior.

I believe that it's not the tools that are divergent. It's the people.

But apparently there's no middle ground.

However, even the most ardent R user might find himself wishing that he could access the Object Oriented capabilities of Python.

Similarly, the most rabid Python user might yearn for access to the wide range of statistical distributions available in R.

Presenting R and Python as opposing solutions is a flawed dichotomy: there is no reason why they should be adversaries. Ideally they should be working together, side by side, hand in hand, to help you achieve your analytics goals.
